Operational Plan

Agricultural Practices

  • Thorough soil testing targeting optimal pH of 5.5-6.5
  • Land clearing followed by plowing 8-10 inches deep
  • Application of organic matter to improve soil structure
  • Raised beds formation in areas with poor drainage
  • Double/triple row planting for efficient land use
  • 30cm between plants, 60-90cm between rows
  • Suckers planted 10-15cm deep
  • Mulching implementation for moisture conservation
  • Planting timed with beginning of rainy season
  • Drip irrigation system for water efficiency
  • Initial manure/compost application (10-15 tons/hectare)
  • Regular NPK fertilizer application (15:15:15)
  • Foliar feeding at critical growth stages
  • Micronutrient supplementation based on soil tests

Legal and Regulatory Compliance

Important Licenses and Permits

  • Certificate of Incorporation (LLC) - Required to establish your company as a legal entity in Kenya
  • KRA PIN Certificate & Tax Compliance - Mandatory for all business operations and tax filing
  • Class B Permit (Agriculture & Husbandry) - Required for foreign investors in agricultural businesses
  • HCD License & AFA Registration - Horticultural Crops Directorate license for crop cultivation
  • Water Resource Use Permit - For irrigation and processing water usage
  • Food Processing License - From Ministry of Health for processing facilities
  • Export License & Phytosanitary Certificate - For international shipments of fresh and processed products

Compliance Requirements

Food Safety Standards

HACCP Certification

Hazard Analysis Critical Control Points system implementation required for processing facility.

Kenya Bureau of Standards

Compliance with KS EAS 770:2011 for fresh fruits and KS EAS 41:2000 for processed fruit products.

Global G.A.P Certification

Recommended for export markets, especially for EU destinations.

Environmental Compliance

  • Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) from NEMA required before establishing processing facility
  • Waste Management Plan must be approved and implemented according to local regulations
  • Water Use Permit from Water Resources Authority for irrigation and processing
  • Carbon Footprint Reporting increasingly important for export markets and ESG compliance
